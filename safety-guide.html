Student Safety Checklist

Use this checklist when viewing properties to ensure your safety and comfort.

Property Evaluation

Secure locks on all doors and windows

Check that locks are functional and secure

Working smoke detectors and fire extinguishers

Ensure safety equipment is present and functional

Proper lighting in common areas and entryways

Well-lit areas improve security, especially at night

Emergency exits clearly marked and accessible

Know your escape routes in case of emergency

Neighborhood Assessment

Safe neighborhood with low crime rates

Research the area or ask locals about safety

Proximity to campus and public transportation

Shorter commutes mean less time traveling alone

Nearby amenities like grocery stores and pharmacies

Convenient access to essentials improves quality of life

Well-maintained streets and public spaces

Clean, well-kept areas often indicate safer neighborhoods

Red Flags to Watch For

Pressure to sign quickly

Legitimate landlords allow time for consideration

Unwillingness to provide documentation

Transparency is key to trustworthy rentals

Cash-only payments

Always use traceable payment methods

Poor maintenance

Neglected properties may have hidden safety issues

Vague or inconsistent information

Trustworthy listings provide clear, consistent details
